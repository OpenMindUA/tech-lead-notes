# Preserve and optimize energy and resources

**TL;DR:** Project resources *and* the PM's decision-making energy
are both finite. Optimize for high-leverage work and protect mental
bandwidth.

## Core ideas

- **80/20 (Pareto).** Aim for 80% of the benefits at 20% of the
  effort, instead of chasing 100% at 100%.
- **Decision fatigue is real.** Energy spent micromanaging is energy
  not available for true PM responsibilities. Delegate routine
  decisions; reserve mental capacity for the high-impact ones.
- **Same logic applies team-wide.** Help team members preserve their
  energy too — minimize ceremony cost and unnecessary involvement.

## When this applies

- Tailoring decisions: cut activities whose marginal benefit is low
- Choosing whether to micromanage vs. delegate
- Designing meeting cadence and review depth
- Saying "no" to elaborate processes that won't return their cost

## Anti-patterns

- Pursuing 100% completeness in every PM artifact
- PM as decision bottleneck for routine matters
- Bloated ceremonies "for the sake of the methodology"
