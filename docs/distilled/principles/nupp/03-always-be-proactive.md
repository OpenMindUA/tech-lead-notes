# Always be proactive

**TL;DR:** Reactive behavior is the default. Project work consistently
benefits from proactive action — reach out before problems escalate.

## Core ideas

- **Reactive default is biological.** It saves energy and is rational
  in domains where you have no leverage. Projects are different —
  you have leverage.
- **Risk management is the canonical example.** Identify and respond
  to uncertainties *before* they materialize.
- Other domains: stakeholder engagement, quality (prevention vs.
  inspection), proactive communication, scope clarification.

## When this applies

- Setting up risk management practices
- Quality planning (prevention over inspection)
- Stakeholder communications (don't wait for complaints)
- Scope clarification before work starts

## Anti-patterns

- Hero-mode firefighting culture
- Risk register only updated after an incident
- "Let's wait and see" on early-warning signals
