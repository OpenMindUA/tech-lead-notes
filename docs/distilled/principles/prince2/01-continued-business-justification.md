# Continued business justification

**TL;DR:** Re-check the project's business case throughout — at
predefined gates. Things change; sunk cost is not a reason to continue.

## Core ideas

- A project that *was* justifiable can stop being so as conditions
  change.
- Two failure modes: blindness to the change, and **sunk cost
  fallacy** (continuing because of past investment).
- Mitigation: predefined, recurring **gates** where the team
  explicitly asks "should we continue, given the latest data?"
- Even when justification holds, gates re-anchor the team on *why*
  the project exists and keep activities aligned.

## When this applies

- Designing a stage-gate process for a project
- Mid-project pivots, scope shocks, market shifts
- Portfolio review (does this project still belong in the mix?)

## Anti-patterns

- One-time business case at kickoff, never reviewed
- Continuing because "we've already spent so much"
- Treating gate reviews as compliance theater
