# PRINCE2® principles — distilled

Seven principles that **support the PRINCE2 methodology**. Mostly
applicable to all projects, but designed as a coherent set with the
PRINCE2 method (covered separately in [Methodologies](../../methodologies/prince2.md)).

| # | Principle | One-liner |
|---|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | [Continued business justification](01-continued-business-justification.md) | Re-check the business case at predefined gates; avoid sunk-cost continuation. |
| 2 | [Learn from experience](02-learn-from-experience.md) | Use existing PM systems and prior project lessons; document and share. |
| 3 | [Defined roles and responsibilities](03-roles.md) | Beyond very small projects, people need to know what's expected and from whom. |
| 4 | [Manage by stages](04-manage-by-stages.md) | High-level plan up-front; detailed plan only for the next stage. |
| 5 | [Manage by exception](05-manage-by-exception.md) | Delegate within tolerances; escalate only when thresholds are breached. |
| 6 | [Focus on products](06-focus-on-products.md) | Plan around what is produced (products), not around activities. |
| 7 | [Tailor to suit the project](07-tailor-to-project.md) | PRINCE2 (and any system) must be tailored, then continuously adapted. |
