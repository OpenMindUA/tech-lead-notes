# Effectively engage with stakeholders

**TL;DR:** A stakeholder is *anyone* with interest who can impact
the project (not just the customer). Identify them aggressively,
communicate often, and tailor the channel per group.

## Core ideas

- **Definition matters.** Stakeholder = any person/group with
  interest in the project who can impact it: team, client,
  suppliers, end users, regulators, competitors, the public.
- **Identification is hard, do it with the team.** Group
  identification surfaces stakeholders a PM working alone misses;
  doubles as team-building.
- **Communicate frequently and proactively.** Different audiences
  need different channels (online dashboards, on-site visits,
  in-person meetings, written reports). "Working well is not
  enough; let others know how well you work."
- **Hostile stakeholders can become champions.** Approach with
  curiosity (ask them to list everything that could go wrong, take
  notes without rebutting). Return with responses to their concerns
  and ask for feedback. Power-holders converted this way bring
  resources and political support.
- **Engagement = decision power.** Delegate decisions within bounds.
  PRINCE2 *manage by exception*: each level acts within thresholds
  on time/cost/quality/risk/benefit; escalate only when exceeded.
- **Use methodology rhythms.** P3.express monthly stakeholder
  satisfaction evaluation + *focused communication* (predefined
  audience-specific messages at process milestones).

## When this applies

- Project initiation: stakeholder register
- Conflict, scope changes, disgruntled groups
- Setting up communication plans and reporting cadences
- Defining decision-authority thresholds (manage by exception)

## Anti-patterns

- Treating "stakeholder" as a synonym for "customer"
- One channel for everyone (e.g. email-only for non-tech audiences)
- Ignoring negative stakeholders or labeling them "always negative"
- PM as bottleneck for every decision (no delegation)
- Reactive comms: waiting until stakeholders complain
