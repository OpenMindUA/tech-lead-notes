# Optimize risk responses

**TL;DR:** A *risk* is any uncertainty (positive or negative) that
can impact the project. Manage risks proactively through layered
responses, continuously, with cost-justified investments.

## Core ideas

- **Definition.** Risk = an uncertainty that can affect the project,
  *either way*. Distinct from certainties (managed by the plan).
- **Identification is continuous, not one-shot.** Start with
  workshops at kickoff, then keep looking. Practice: a short fixed
  agenda item at the end of every meeting to surface new risks.
  Risks emerge as the project progresses and the environment shifts.
- **Layered response design** (concrete safety example):
    1. Add an activity to the plan that prevents the risk
       (build temporary handrails immediately when a floor is laid)
    2. Adjust the plan to reduce exposure
       (limit work on unfinished floors until permanent walls)
    3. Capacity-building responses that cover *several* risks at
       once (health-and-safety training)
    4. Reduce *impact* of residual risk if it occurs
       (on-site nurse / first aid)
    5. Transfer/finance residual risk
       (insurance policy)
- **Investment must be justified.** Pay for risk responses only when
  the response cost is justified by the expected reduction in risk.
  (See system-interactions principle on EV reasoning.)
- **Why this matters.** Proper risk management reduces cost,
  protects reputation, fulfills social responsibility, and improves
  project predictability.

## When this applies

- Project initiation: initial risk register
- Recurring meeting cadence (add risk-scan to closing 5 minutes)
- Designing safety plans / quality plans / contingency plans
- Deciding whether to buy insurance and at what coverage
- Evaluating mitigations: pick highest-coverage-per-cost responses

## Anti-patterns

- One-time risk identification at kickoff, never revisited
- Single-layer responses (only insurance, or only training)
- Treating risk only as negative (missing positive risks)
- Risk-response cost greater than the risk itself reduces
- "We covered safety in onboarding" without a project-specific plan
