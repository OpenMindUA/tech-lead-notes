# Focus on value

**TL;DR:** Value is the **benefits-to-cost ratio** (not "benefits"
alone). Every project decision must be traceable to *why* the
project exists. Value lives at the portfolio layer; projects align
to it and feed it information.

## Core ideas

- **Decisions need the "why".** A trade-off between time/quality/cost
  cannot be answered by the project team in isolation; it depends on
  whether the project's purpose is, for example, market entry
  (preserve quality, spend more) or revenue (cut quality if needed).
  Escalate such decisions to the level that owns the why.
- **Justification ≠ Value.** PRINCE2 frames the "why" as
  *business case justification*, continually re-checked. PMBOK 7
  uses *value*, with justification nested inside.
- **Definition: value = benefits ÷ cost.** A €1,000-benefit project
  costing €100 is twice as valuable as one costing €200. Beware:
  "value" in everyday speech often means "benefit"; mixing the two
  causes bad decisions.
- **Benefits are subjective and broader than money.** Lives saved,
  environmental impact, reputation, market entry, capability
  building. Each organization has its own *value drivers*.
- **Value is holistic and cross-temporal.** A project's value
  depends on adjacent projects, organizational strategy, regulation,
  even future products. Outputs can amplify each other (e.g. one
  product launch lifts sales of others).
- **Short vs long term depends on context.** Same investment, lump
  benefit now vs annuity-style return for 20 years — choice depends
  on cash position and existing portfolio mix.
- **Value is owned by portfolio management.** That layer selects and
  prioritizes projects. Project layer must (a) align activities to
  value drivers and (b) provide up-to-date data so portfolio can
  re-evaluate.

## When this applies

- Major scope/quality/schedule trade-offs (escalate to owner of why)
- Drafting/refreshing the business case
- Portfolio prioritization and project selection
- Reporting up: surface data that lets portfolio re-evaluate
- Choosing between short-term and long-term opportunities

## Anti-patterns

- Treating "value" as synonymous with "benefit"
- Project-local optimization that ignores org/portfolio context
- Static business case that is never re-checked after kickoff
- PM unilaterally trades quality for time without escalation
- Value drivers undefined → every stakeholder optimizes their own
