# Recognize, evaluate, and respond to system interactions

**TL;DR:** Decisions ripple through interconnected systems and are
distorted by cognitive biases. Reason holistically and apply
critical thinking, not gut feel.

## Core ideas

- **Systems thinking = critical thinking + holistic thinking.**
  Reason about non-linear interactions; the project's outcome
  depends on org context, other projects, future plans, regulation.
- **Use expected-value reasoning across many decisions.** A single
  trial doesn't matter — the strategy across thousands of similar
  decisions does. Pick the higher EV consistently.
- **EV is conditional on risk appetite.** A high-EV bet is only
  rational if the downside fits within the loss you can bear.
  Money has non-linear utility: losing your last €200 is worse
  than losing one of many €200s.
- **Cognitive biases distort decisions.** Notable ones in projects:
    - **Loss aversion** — we weight losses ~2.5× gains; refuse
      good bets out of fear
    - **Confirmation bias** — seeking only data that supports a hypothesis
    - **Sunk cost fallacy** — continuing because of past investment
    - **Effort justification** — overvaluing what we worked hard on
    - **Planning fallacy** — systematic underestimation of duration/cost
    - **Halo effect**, **illusion of control**, **base rate fallacy**,
      **zero risk bias**, **stereotyping**
- **You can manage biases consciously.** Learn them; build review
  steps and decision frameworks that surface them.

## When this applies

- Major go/no-go decisions with significant downside
- Risk response design (calibration of EV vs. risk appetite)
- Budget/schedule estimation (planning fallacy)
- Pivot/cancel decisions (sunk cost, effort justification)
- Reviewing forecasts that "feel right" without explicit reasoning

## Anti-patterns

- "Gut feel" decisions on high-impact issues
- Refusing positive-EV bets because "we might lose"
- Doubling down on a failing project because of past spend
- Ignoring base rates ("our project will be different")
- Local optimization that ignores org/portfolio context
