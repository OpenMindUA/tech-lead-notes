# PMBOK 7 principles — distilled

Twelve principles that PMBOK 7 uses as a **lens to interpret** project
management situations. They are **not** a methodology and **not** a
checklist of activities — apply them to evaluate decisions and tailor
methods.

| # | Principle | One-liner |
|---|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | [Stewardship](01-stewardship.md) | The PM is a facilitator and steward, not a technical boss. |
| 2 | [Collaborative team](02-collaborative-team.md) | Build a team, not a collection of stars; embed team-building in real work. |
| 3 | [Engage stakeholders](03-engage-stakeholders.md) | Identify everyone who can impact the project; communicate frequently and tailored. |
| 4 | [Focus on value](04-focus-on-value.md) | Value = benefits ÷ cost; align every decision with the project's "why". |
| 5 | [System interactions](05-system-interactions.md) | Decisions ripple; evaluate consequences across the whole system, not in isolation. |
| 6 | [Leadership](06-leadership.md) | Leadership is a behavior, not a role; anyone can lead in their domain. |
| 7 | [Tailor based on context](07-tailor.md) | No universal method; adapt to project, organization, environment. |
| 8 | [Build quality in](08-quality.md) | Quality is built into processes and deliverables, not inspected at the end. |
| 9 | [Optimize risk responses](09-risk.md) | Spend on risk responses only when their cost is justified by the risk reduction. |
| 10 | [Navigate complexity](10-complexity.md) | Recognize complexity sources; respond with simplification and adaptation. |
| 11 | [Adaptability and resiliency](11-adaptability.md) | Build the capacity to handle and recover from change. |
| 12 | [Enable change](12-enable-change.md) | Project outputs only create value when stakeholders adopt them; manage adoption. |
