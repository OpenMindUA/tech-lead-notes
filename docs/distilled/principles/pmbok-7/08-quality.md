# Build quality into processes and deliverables

**TL;DR:** Quality is *prevention*, integrated into the system, not
final inspection bolted on. It applies to the *product* and to the
*PM system itself*. Investment must be net-positive; if not, the
quality system is poorly tailored.

## Core ideas

- **Net-positive cost-benefit.** "Quality is too expensive" means it
  isn't tailored. Effective quality work reduces rework cost,
  improves reputation, and saves time overall.
- **Prevention over inspection.** Final testing is necessary but
  insufficient. The main goal is to prevent defects, not catch
  them. (This aligns with NUPP "always be proactive".)
- **Integrate, don't bolt on.** Quality as an afterthought is
  ineffective. Build it into every process.
- **Two layers to manage:**
    1. Quality of the **product** and its production processes.
    2. Quality of the **PM system** itself.
- **Product quality is domain-specific.** PM doesn't need to know
  every method, but must ensure they are *identified, documented,
  executed*. Sources of requirements: customer approval, internal
  organization standards, national/regulatory codes.
    - IT example: automated/manual tests + pair programming for
      proactive defect prevention
    - Construction example: destructive and non-destructive concrete
      tests + cement/sand/water mix specs + curing procedures
- **Managerial quality via peer reviews.** P3.express recommends a
  PM peer review at intervals, ideally with a *different* reviewer
  each time to maximize diversity of feedback. Both parties learn.

## When this applies

- Drafting a quality plan
- Pushback that "quality costs too much" (test net cost-benefit)
- Choosing between proactive (e.g. pair programming) and reactive
  (testing only) quality methods
- Building review cadence into the PM's own work, not just the team's

## Anti-patterns

- Quality = final testing only
- Standalone "quality department" disconnected from delivery
- Untailored quality plan more expensive than the savings it brings
- No mechanism to evaluate the *PM's* own work quality
- Same peer reviewer every time → narrow feedback
