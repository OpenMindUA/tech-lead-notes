# Glossary

Distilled definitions of recurring terms, derived from the book.
Each entry links to the primary distilled card or full chunk that
develops it.

## A

### Adaptive (development approach)
Build subsets of the output, expose to end users, collect feedback,
decide next step. Right when the output's value depends on user
acceptance. **Requires usable increments** (otherwise feedback is
unreliable).
→ [Development approach domain](performance-domains/03-development-approach.md) ·
[Adaptability principle](principles/pmbok-7/11-adaptability.md)

### Agile
Common label for adaptive methods. PMBOK 7 avoids the label
"waterfall" for predictive methods because of its negative
connotations.
→ [Adaptability principle](principles/pmbok-7/11-adaptability.md)

### Artifact
A managed item produced or used in a methodology
(register, plan, document, board, etc.). Can be physical or
digital. Tailoring decides which artifacts you actually need.

## B

### Benefits
The positive results an organization seeks from a project. Can be
monetary or non-monetary (lives saved, reputation, market entry,
capability building). **Subjective** — depends on the
organization's *value drivers*.
→ [Focus on value principle](principles/pmbok-7/04-focus-on-value.md)

### Business case
Document of the project's justification (PRINCE2 term). Must be
re-checked throughout the project, not only at kickoff.
→ [Continued business justification](principles/prince2/01-continued-business-justification.md)

## C

### Cargo Cult (effect)
Imitating the surface form of a practice without understanding the
underlying purpose; producing no benefit. The book repeatedly
warns against cargo-cult adoption of methodologies and templates.
→ [Don't do anything without a clear purpose](principles/nupp/05-clear-purpose.md)

### Centralized vs distributed PM
- **Centralized:** dedicated PM team and a PM on top.
- **Distributed:** PM activities spread across all team members
  (no PM role). Works only at small team size.
Project *management* always exists; the *project manager* role is
optional.
→ [Team domain](performance-domains/02-team.md)

### Change request
A request to alter scope/requirements/etc. Must be impact-assessed
(time/cost/quality), turned into proposal options, decided. Change
is fine *when managed*.
→ [Project Work domain](performance-domains/05-project-work.md)

### Cognitive bias
Systematic deviations from rational decision-making. Notable in
projects: loss aversion, confirmation bias, sunk cost fallacy,
planning fallacy, halo effect, illusion of control, etc. Manage
them via critical thinking.
→ [System interactions principle](principles/pmbok-7/05-system-interactions.md)

### Critical Path Method (CPM)
Dependency-based scheduling — calculate order/start dates from a
network of activity dependencies. Tools: Primavera P6, MS Project.
→ [Planning domain](performance-domains/04-planning.md)

### Custodian (P3.express)
A named person responsible for tracking a follow-up item or
deliverable through to closure. Prevents items from being lost.
→ [P3.express](methodologies/p3-express.md)

## D

### Deliverable
A specific output produced by the project; building element of
the final product. Hierarchical breakdown (WBS / PBS /
deliverables map) avoids unmanageable flat lists.
→ [Delivery domain](performance-domains/07-delivery.md)

### Definition of Done (DoD)
Common acceptance criteria across items in Agile/Scrum. Items not
satisfying DoD return to the backlog.
→ [Scrum](methodologies/scrum.md) ·
[Delivery domain](performance-domains/07-delivery.md)

### DSDM Coach
A dedicated role in DSDM that helps teams use the methodology.
Notable because most methods leave methodology coaching unassigned —
which the author flags as a problem.
→ [DSDM](methodologies/dsdm.md) ·
[Tailor principle (PMBOK 7)](principles/pmbok-7/07-tailor.md)

## E

### Executive (PRINCE2)
Senior business decision-maker, head of the Project Board. Owns
project outcomes; makes high-level decisions. Equivalent role
elsewhere: Sponsor (P3.express), Business Sponsor (DSDM).
→ [PRINCE2](methodologies/prince2.md) ·
[Team domain](performance-domains/02-team.md)

### Expected value (EV)
Probability-weighted outcome. Strategy: pick higher EV across
many decisions, conditional on risk appetite (do not bet money
you cannot afford to lose).
→ [System interactions principle](principles/pmbok-7/05-system-interactions.md)

## F

### Focused communication (P3.express)
Short, defined messages sent to predefined audiences at process
milestones. Keeps everyone informed without requiring stakeholders
to ask.
→ [P3.express](methodologies/p3-express.md) ·
[Engage stakeholders](principles/pmbok-7/03-engage-stakeholders.md)

### Follow-up item / Follow-up register (P3.express)
An item that needs tracking — risk, issue, change request,
improvement plan, or lesson learned. P3.express stores all five
types in a single register.
→ [Project Work domain](performance-domains/05-project-work.md) ·
[P3.express](methodologies/p3-express.md)

## I

### Increment
A *usable* set of deliverables produced by an iteration of an
adaptive project. Required for meaningful feedback. All
increments are deliverable sets, but not all deliverable sets are
increments.
→ [Delivery domain](performance-domains/07-delivery.md)

### Information radiator
A physical board displaying status data where colocated team
members see it. Online equivalent: dashboard. Don't assume people
will check on their own — push notifications too.
→ [Measurement domain](performance-domains/06-measurement.md)

### Issue
An unplanned event that has actually happened (vs. *risk*, which
is uncertain).
→ [Project Work domain](performance-domains/05-project-work.md)

## L

### Lessons learned
Observations from project experience captured for future projects.
Capture during the project, not only at close.
→ [Project Work domain](performance-domains/05-project-work.md) ·
[Learn from experience principle](principles/prince2/02-learn-from-experience.md)

### Loss aversion
Cognitive bias: losses are weighted ~2.5× gains. Causes refusal of
positive-EV bets.
→ [System interactions principle](principles/pmbok-7/05-system-interactions.md)

## M

### Manage by exception (PRINCE2)
Delegate decisions within tolerances; escalate only when
thresholds are breached. Saves senior energy, creates buy-in,
faster decisions.
→ [Manage by exception principle](principles/prince2/05-manage-by-exception.md)

### Manage by stages (PRINCE2)
High-level plan up-front, detailed plan only for the next stage.
Aligns with continued business justification (gates).
→ [Manage by stages principle](principles/prince2/04-manage-by-stages.md)

### Meta-plan
Document describing *how* you'll plan/monitor/control a domain
(e.g. *risk management plan*) — distinct from the plan artifact
itself (e.g. *risk register*).
→ [Planning domain](performance-domains/04-planning.md)

### Monte Carlo analysis
Probabilistic technique: sample inputs to estimate the probability
distribution of outcomes (e.g. probability of finishing by month
22). Resource-heavy — use only when warranted.
→ [Uncertainty domain](performance-domains/08-uncertainty.md)

### MoSCoW
Prioritization technique used in DSDM:
- **M** — Must-have (≤ 60%)
- **S** — Should-have
- **C** — Could-have (≥ 20%)
- **W** — Won't-have-this-time
→ [DSDM](methodologies/dsdm.md)

## O

### Outcome
What changes in the world / org as a result of using the project's
output. Distinct from output. Outcome management is primarily
program-level work, but the project must align and report.
→ [Enable change principle](principles/pmbok-7/12-enable-change.md)

### Output
What the project builds (the product). Distinct from outcome.
Outputs only matter when adopted and producing outcomes.
→ [Enable change principle](principles/pmbok-7/12-enable-change.md)

## P

### Pareto / 80-20 rule
Aim for 80% of benefits at 20% of effort.
→ [Preserve energy principle](principles/nupp/02-preserve-energy.md)

### Peer review (P3.express)
Another PM reviews your work and gives feedback. Prefer a
different reviewer each time. Both parties learn.
→ [P3.express](methodologies/p3-express.md) ·
[Build quality in](principles/pmbok-7/08-quality.md)

### Performance domain (PMBOK 7)
A topic area you must cover with your methodology (Stakeholders,
Team, Development approach, Planning, Project Work, Measurement,
Delivery, Uncertainty). Use them to verify and enrich your method.
→ [Performance domains index](performance-domains/index.md)

### PMO
"Project Management Office" — a vague organizational term. Can be
useful as a *Center of Excellence* hosting reusable meta-plans.
Bad when it duplicates project-level PM.
→ [Planning domain](performance-domains/04-planning.md)

### Portfolio management
The org-level layer that selects and prioritizes projects (and
manages cross-project risks). PM = doing things right; portfolio
management = doing the right things.
→ [Focus on value principle](principles/pmbok-7/04-focus-on-value.md) ·
[Uncertainty domain](performance-domains/08-uncertainty.md)

### Predictive (development approach)
Plan and design the whole output up-front, then build. Right when
the output is fully knowable in advance.
→ [Development approach domain](performance-domains/03-development-approach.md)

### Product Backlog (Scrum)
Value-ordered list of items to do; managed by the Product Owner;
evolves throughout the project.
→ [Scrum](methodologies/scrum.md)

### Product Owner (Scrum)
Single business-side person; orders the Product Backlog; main
hub between team and other stakeholders.
→ [Scrum](methodologies/scrum.md)

### Program management
Coordination layer above projects, owning outcomes that require
multiple projects + change-management activities.
→ [Enable change principle](principles/pmbok-7/12-enable-change.md)

### Project Initiation Documentation (PID, PRINCE2)
Comprehensive baseline created in the *initiating a project*
process. Includes refined business case and detailed targets.
→ [PRINCE2](methodologies/prince2.md)

## R

### Risk
Any uncertainty that may impact the project, positively or
negatively. Distinct from issues (already happened) and
certainties (managed by the plan).
→ [Optimize risk responses](principles/pmbok-7/09-risk.md) ·
[Uncertainty domain](performance-domains/08-uncertainty.md)

### Risk register
Spreadsheet/table of risks with analysis and planned responses.
Used in PRINCE2 and many others. P3.express keeps risks in the
combined *follow-up register* instead.
→ [Uncertainty domain](performance-domains/08-uncertainty.md)

## S

### Scrum Master
Scrum role: ensures proper use of Scrum, facilitates, unblocks.
Focus on context, not content.
→ [Scrum](methodologies/scrum.md)

### Sponsor
Senior business owner of the project (P3.express term). Provides
resources, makes high-level decisions. Equivalents: Executive
(PRINCE2), Business Sponsor (DSDM).
→ [Team domain](performance-domains/02-team.md)

### Sprint
Fixed-duration cycle (≤ 1 month) in Scrum. Ends on time even if
items unfinished; unfinished items return to backlog.
→ [Scrum](methodologies/scrum.md)

### Stakeholder
Any person/group with interest in the project who can impact it —
team members, clients, suppliers, end users, regulators,
competitors, public. **Not** just the customer.
→ [Engage stakeholders principle](principles/pmbok-7/03-engage-stakeholders.md) ·
[Stakeholders domain](performance-domains/01-stakeholders.md)

### Stakeholder register
Documented list of stakeholders with analysis and engagement
plans. Some methods provide a dedicated artifact; others embed it
in a more general one.
→ [Stakeholders domain](performance-domains/01-stakeholders.md)

### Sunk cost fallacy
Bias to continue an activity because of past investment. PRINCE2
*continued business justification* explicitly counters it through
gate reviews.
→ [Continued business justification](principles/prince2/01-continued-business-justification.md)

### Systems thinking (PMBOK 7)
Critical thinking + holistic thinking. Reasoning about complex,
non-linear interactions in projects.
→ [System interactions principle](principles/pmbok-7/05-system-interactions.md)

## T

### Tailoring
Adapting a methodology to the project context. *Not* cherry-picking
from multiple frameworks (that produces inconsistent
"Frankenstein" systems). Tailoring is system design.
→ [Tailor principle (PMBOK 7)](principles/pmbok-7/07-tailor.md) ·
[Tailor to suit principle (PRINCE2)](principles/prince2/07-tailor-to-project.md)

### Timebox (DSDM)
Fixed total project duration; deliver as much as possible inside
it.
→ [DSDM](methodologies/dsdm.md)

### Tolerance (PRINCE2)
Threshold for time/cost/quality/scope/risk/benefit at a management
level. Within tolerance → that level decides; beyond → escalate.
→ [Manage by exception principle](principles/prince2/05-manage-by-exception.md)

## U

### User story
Format used in XP / Agile to express a requirement:
"As a [role], I want [action] so that [reason]." The "so that"
expresses the *why* and supports better solution choices.
→ [Delivery domain](performance-domains/07-delivery.md)

## V

### Value
**Benefits-to-cost ratio** (PMBOK 7 sense). NOT synonymous with
"benefit" alone. Use the ratio for project comparisons.
→ [Focus on value principle](principles/pmbok-7/04-focus-on-value.md)

### Value driver
Something that an organization actually values (revenue, lives
saved, reputation, market position, …). Differs by org.
Determines what counts as value in their projects.
→ [Focus on value principle](principles/pmbok-7/04-focus-on-value.md)

## W

### Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
Hierarchical decomposition of project deliverables. Naming
varies: WBS (many), Product Breakdown Structure (PRINCE2),
Deliverables Map (P3.express).
→ [Delivery domain](performance-domains/07-delivery.md)

### Work-in-progress (WIP) limit
Cap on the number of items being worked simultaneously. Lean
origin, broadly applicable. Optimal parallelism is usually lower
than people assume.
→ [Delivery domain](performance-domains/07-delivery.md)
