# Sprint Backlog (+ Sprint Goal)

**TL;DR:** A plan **by and for the Developers**. Equation:
**Sprint Goal (why) + selected Product Backlog items (what) + plan
to deliver (how)**. Its **commitment** is the **Sprint Goal**.

## Composition

- **Sprint Goal** — the **why**.
- **Selected Product Backlog items** — the **what**.
- **Actionable plan for delivering** the Increment — the **how**.

## Properties

- **By and for the Developers** — they author and own it.
- **Highly visible, real-time picture** of the Sprint's planned work.
- **Updated throughout the Sprint** as more is learned.
- Should have **enough detail** to inspect progress in the **Daily
  Scrum**.

## Commitment: Sprint Goal

- **Single objective** for the Sprint.
- **Commitment by the Developers**, but provides **flexibility** in
  how the work is done.
- Creates **coherence and focus** — encourages working together
  rather than on separate initiatives.
- **Created during Sprint Planning** and added to the Sprint Backlog.
- **Kept in mind throughout the Sprint** as work progresses.
- If work turns out different than expected, the team **negotiates
  scope** of the Sprint Backlog **with the PO** — *without* changing
  the Sprint Goal.

## When this applies

- Designing the Sprint Backlog format/tooling
- Mid-Sprint scope renegotiation (preserve Goal, change scope)
- Daily inspection of progress in the Daily Scrum

## Anti-patterns

- Sprint Backlog **owned by PO or SM** instead of Developers
- **No Sprint Goal** — Sprint becomes a list of disconnected items
- Multiple Sprint Goals — splits focus
- Sprint Goal **changed mid-Sprint** instead of keeping the goal
  fixed and renegotiating scope
- Sprint Backlog frozen at planning, no daily updates
- Sprint Backlog kept in a tool with limited team visibility
