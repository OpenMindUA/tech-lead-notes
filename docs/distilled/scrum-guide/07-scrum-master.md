# Scrum Master

**TL;DR:** The Scrum Master is **accountable for establishing Scrum
as defined in the Guide** and **for the Scrum Team's
effectiveness**. A **true leader who serves** — the team, the
Product Owner, and the wider organization.

## Two top-level accountabilities

1. **Establish Scrum** (Guide-correct) by helping everyone — team
   and organization — understand Scrum theory and practice.
2. **Team effectiveness** by enabling the team to improve its
   practices within the Scrum framework.

## Three categories of service

### Serves the **Scrum Team**
- Coach the team in **self-management** and **cross-functionality**.
- Help the team focus on creating high-value Increments meeting
  the **Definition of Done**.
- Cause **removal of impediments** to progress.
- Ensure all Scrum events take place, are positive and productive,
  and stay within the **timebox**.

### Serves the **Product Owner**
- Help find techniques for **effective Product Goal definition** and
  **Product Backlog management**.
- Help the team understand the need for **clear and concise**
  Product Backlog items.
- Help establish **empirical product planning** for complex
  environments.
- Facilitate **stakeholder collaboration** as requested or needed.

### Serves the **organization**
- **Lead, train, coach** the organization in its Scrum adoption.
- **Plan and advise** Scrum implementations.
- Help everyone understand and enact an empirical approach for
  complex work.
- **Remove barriers** between stakeholders and Scrum Teams.

## When this applies

- Hiring or appointing a Scrum Master
- Setting Scrum Master objectives / performance reviews
- Resolving conflicts between team and external stakeholders
- Diagnosing why a Scrum adoption is stuck (which service is
  missing?)

## Anti-patterns

- **Scrum Master as secretary / note-taker** for the team
- **Scrum Master as boss** — telling Developers what to do
- **Solo Scrum Master** focused only inside the team — ignores
  the Product Owner and organizational service
- **Permanent impediment list** with no removal action
- **Skipping Scrum events** "to save time" — violates the
  cadence-establishing accountability
- **Tolerating** organization-level violations of Scrum (e.g.
  Backlog reordered by a manager) instead of coaching against them
