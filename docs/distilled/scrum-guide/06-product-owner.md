# Product Owner

**TL;DR:** The Product Owner is **accountable for maximizing product
value** and for **effective Product Backlog management**.
**One person, not a committee.** May delegate, but accountability stays.

## Accountabilities

- Maximize the value of the product resulting from the team's work.
- Effective Product Backlog management:
    - **Develop and explicitly communicate the Product Goal.**
    - **Create and clearly communicate Product Backlog items.**
    - **Order Product Backlog items.**
    - **Ensure the Backlog is transparent, visible and understood.**

The Product Owner *may* delegate any of the above; **regardless,
the Product Owner remains accountable**.

## Authority and respect

For the Product Owner to succeed, the **entire organization must
respect their decisions**. Decisions are visible:
- in the **content and ordering** of the Product Backlog;
- through the **inspectable Increment** at the Sprint Review.

People who want to change the Backlog **convince the Product Owner**.
The Product Owner does not negotiate with everyone individually.

## When this applies

- Defining Product Owner job description
- Disputes about backlog priority — answered by PO authority
- Clarifying who can change scope mid-Sprint (only PO)
- Handling "many stakeholders, one product" — PO is the single hub

## Anti-patterns

- **Committee Product Owner** — multiple people sharing the
  accountability ("Proxy PO", "PO team")
- PO without organizational backing — others overrule the order
- PO as **scribe** — only writing down what stakeholders dictate
- Organization treating PO orders as advisory
- Stakeholders bypassing PO to talk directly to Developers about
  scope
