# Product Backlog (+ Product Goal)

**TL;DR:** **Single source of work** undertaken by the Scrum Team —
emergent, ordered list. Its **commitment** is the **Product Goal**:
the long-term objective the team plans against.

## Properties

- **Single source of work** for the Scrum Team — nothing the team
  does originates outside it.
- **Emergent** — evolves continuously.
- **Ordered** — the PO decides ordering (see PO card).
- **Refinement** is **ongoing**: break items down, add description,
  order, size. Attributes vary by domain.
- An item is **"ready"** for Sprint Planning when the team can Done
  it within one Sprint — typically achieved through refinement.
- **Sizing is owned by Developers** (those who will do the work).
  PO may help with trade-offs.

## Commitment: Product Goal

- **Long-term objective** of the Scrum Team.
- **Lives inside** the Product Backlog.
- The rest of the Backlog **emerges to define "what" will fulfill**
  the Goal.
- A team **fulfills (or abandons)** one Product Goal **before**
  taking on the next.
- *"A product is a vehicle to deliver value. It has a clear
  boundary, known stakeholders, well-defined users or customers.
  A product could be a service, a physical product, or something
  more abstract."*

## When this applies

- Setting up a new product / new team
- Coaching teams on refinement (when, how often, who)
- Resolving "how big is this item?" disputes — Developers size
- Aligning stakeholders around the Product Goal

## Anti-patterns

- **No Product Goal** — Backlog becomes a wishlist
- **Multiple parallel Product Goals** — splits team focus
- Backlog ordered by anyone other than the PO
- Items dropped into Sprint Planning **without refinement** —
  forecasts become guesses
- PO sizes items instead of Developers
- "Backlog grooming" as one-off event rather than ongoing activity
