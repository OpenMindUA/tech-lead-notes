# Daily Scrum

**TL;DR:** **15-minute event for Developers**. Inspect progress
toward the Sprint Goal; produce an actionable plan for the next day.

## Properties

- **Timebox: 15 minutes**, every working day of the Sprint.
- **Same time and place** every day to reduce complexity.
- **For the Developers.** PO and SM attend only as Developers — i.e.
  if they are actively working on Sprint Backlog items.
- **No prescribed format.** Developers choose technique and structure
  as long as they:
    - focus on **progress toward the Sprint Goal**, and
    - produce an **actionable plan for the next day**.

## Effects

- Improves **communication**.
- Surfaces **impediments**.
- Drives **quick decisions**.
- Eliminates the need for many other meetings.

## Not the only adjustment point

Developers may meet **throughout the day** for more detailed
discussions about adapting or replanning the Sprint's remaining
work. The Daily Scrum is the **minimum** cadence, not the only
chance.

## When this applies

- Daily replanning of who-does-what against the Sprint Goal
- Surfacing impediments early
- Designing async or distributed-team variants (still 15 min, still
  Sprint-Goal-focused)

## Anti-patterns

- "Status update for the manager" / Scrum Master
- Three-questions ritual (yesterday/today/blockers) without
  reference to the Sprint Goal
- Longer than 15 minutes — split off discussions afterwards
- PO or SM dominating the meeting
- Daily Scrum as the *only* sync — Developers should also meet
  ad-hoc as needed
