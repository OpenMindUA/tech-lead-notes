# Sprint Review

**TL;DR:** Inspect the **outcome** of the Sprint with stakeholders
and decide what to do next. **Working session, not a demo.** Timebox
**4 h** for a one-month Sprint.

## Purpose

- **Inspect** the outcome of the Sprint.
- **Determine future adaptations.**
- **Discuss** progress toward the Product Goal.

## What happens

- Scrum Team and stakeholders **review** what was accomplished and
  what changed in the environment.
- Attendees **collaborate on what to do next**.
- The **Product Backlog may be adjusted** to meet new opportunities.
- Avoid limiting the event to a **presentation** — it is a working
  session.

## Position and timebox

- **Second-to-last** event of the Sprint (Retrospective is last).
- **Up to 4 hours** for a one-month Sprint; less for shorter.

## When this applies

- Every Sprint
- Stakeholder communication / managing expectations
- Adjusting Product Backlog based on new market info
- Demonstrating progress toward the Product Goal

## Anti-patterns

- **One-way demo** — team shows; stakeholders silently watch
- Skipped because "stakeholders aren't available"
- Stakeholders use it to dictate the next Sprint's content (PO
  decides; review is collaborative input)
- Treating the Sprint Review as a release **gate** — release should
  not depend on it (see Increment card)
- "Review" only of what was completed; ignoring environmental
  changes
