# Scrum Team — structure

**TL;DR:** A single small team (≤10), three accountabilities
(Developers, Product Owner, Scrum Master), no sub-teams or
hierarchies, focused on **one** Product Goal at a time.
Cross-functional and self-managing; the entire team is accountable
for a valuable Increment **every Sprint**.

## Properties

- **Composition (immutable).** One Scrum Master, one Product Owner,
  Developers. No additional layers.
- **Size.** Typically **10 or fewer** people. Smaller communicates
  better and is more productive.
- **Cross-functional.** Has **all** skills needed to create value
  each Sprint — no external hand-offs to "complete the work".
- **Self-managing.** Internally decides who does what, when, how.
- **Single product focus.** One Product Goal at a time.
- **End-to-end ownership.** Stakeholder collaboration, verification,
  maintenance, operation, experimentation, research, anything else.
- **Sustainable pace.** Working in Sprints at a sustainable pace
  improves focus and consistency.

## Three accountabilities

- **Developers** — committed to creating any aspect of a usable
  Increment each Sprint. → [card](05-developers.md)
- **Product Owner** — accountable for maximizing product value,
  manages the Product Backlog. → [card](06-product-owner.md)
- **Scrum Master** — accountable for the team's effectiveness and
  for establishing Scrum as defined in the Guide. → [card](07-scrum-master.md)

## Scaling rule

> If teams become too large, **reorganize into multiple cohesive
> Scrum Teams**, each focused on the same product. They share the
> same **Product Goal**, **Product Backlog**, and **Product Owner**.

There is no "Scrum at scale" defined in the Scrum Guide; multi-team
setups are explicit only in this rule.

## When this applies

- Designing a new product team
- Reorganizing existing teams that have grown beyond ~10 people
- Setting up multi-team product (multiple Scrum Teams sharing a
  backlog)
- Defining job descriptions and team boundaries

## Anti-patterns

- Adding a "Project Manager" role on top of the three
  accountabilities
- Splitting into dev/QA/ops sub-teams — kills cross-functional
  ownership
- Multiple Product Owners on the same product
- Single Product Owner managing many disconnected products
  (reverses the focus property)
- Team larger than ~12 → communication overhead drowns
  empiricism
- Team that needs hand-offs to a "different team" to release →
  not cross-functional
