# Increment (+ Definition of Done)

**TL;DR:** A **concrete, usable, additive stepping stone** toward
the Product Goal. Its **commitment** is the **Definition of Done**:
the formal description of "Done" quality. **Multiple Increments**
may be created within a Sprint and **released independently** of
the Sprint Review.

## Increment properties

- **Concrete stepping stone** toward the Product Goal.
- **Additive** to all prior Increments — they all work together.
- **Thoroughly verified.**
- **Must be usable** to provide value.
- **Multiple Increments per Sprint** are possible. The **sum** is
  presented at the Sprint Review (supports empiricism).
- **Can be released before the Sprint Review.** *"The Sprint Review
  should never be considered a gate to releasing value."*

## Commitment: Definition of Done

- **Formal description** of the state of the Increment when it
  meets the **quality measures** required for the product.
- *"The moment a Product Backlog item meets the Definition of Done,
  an Increment is born."*
- Creates **transparency** — everyone has a shared understanding of
  what was completed.
- An item that **doesn't meet DoD** **cannot be released or
  presented at the Sprint Review** — it returns to the Product
  Backlog.

## DoD scope rules

- If the **organization has a DoD standard**, all Scrum Teams must
  follow it as a **minimum**.
- Otherwise, the **Scrum Team creates a DoD appropriate for the
  product**.
- **Developers conform** to the DoD.
- If multiple Scrum Teams work on **one product**, they **mutually
  define and comply with the same DoD**.

## When this applies

- Defining quality criteria for a new product
- Resolving "is this done?" arguments
- Multi-team product setup — single shared DoD
- Choosing release cadence (release per Increment, not per Sprint)

## Anti-patterns

- **No Definition of Done** — "done" becomes subjective
- DoD too weak — Increments ship that aren't actually usable
- DoD frozen forever — should evolve with the product (see retro)
- Treating Sprint Review as a release gate — Increments may release
  earlier
- Multiple teams on one product, **different DoDs** — fragmentation
- "Done-done" / "really done" suffixes — symptom of weak DoD
