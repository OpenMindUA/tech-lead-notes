# Sprint Planning

**TL;DR:** Initiates the Sprint by laying out the work. Whole-team
collaboration. Output = **Sprint Goal + Sprint Backlog**. Timebox
**8 h** for a 1-month Sprint.

## Three topics

### 1. **Why** is this Sprint valuable?
- **Product Owner** proposes how the product can increase value/
  utility this Sprint.
- Whole team collaborates to define the **Sprint Goal**.
- The **Sprint Goal must be finalized before the end of Sprint
  Planning**.

### 2. **What** can be Done this Sprint?
- **Developers** select Product Backlog items in discussion with
  the PO.
- Items may be refined during the discussion (improves understanding
  and confidence).
- Forecast quality grows with knowledge of: past performance,
  upcoming capacity, **Definition of Done**.

### 3. **How** will the chosen work get done?
- **Developers** plan how to turn each selected item into an
  Increment that meets the **Definition of Done**.
- Often by decomposing items into work of **one day or less**.
- **No one else tells Developers how** to do this.

## Output

> **Sprint Backlog** = Sprint Goal + selected Product Backlog items + plan for delivering them.

## Timebox

- **Up to 8 hours** for a one-month Sprint.
- Shorter Sprints → usually shorter planning.

## Participants

- Whole Scrum Team.
- Others may be invited for **advice** (not for command).

## When this applies

- Start of every Sprint
- Re-planning after a major change or cancellation
- Designing planning facilitation for the team

## Anti-patterns

- **No Sprint Goal** at end of planning — most common dysfunction
- PO dictating *how* the Developers will work
- Planning more items than the Definition of Done can sustain
- "We'll figure out the goal later" — invalidates the focus
- Planning skipped because "we know what to do"
- Stakeholders dictating scope at planning instead of advising
