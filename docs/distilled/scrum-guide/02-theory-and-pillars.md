# Scrum theory and the three pillars

**TL;DR:** Scrum is grounded in **empiricism** (knowledge from
experience and observation) and **lean thinking** (reduce waste,
keep the essentials). Empiricism is operationalized through three
pillars — **Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation** — which the five
Scrum events make happen on a fixed cadence.

## The two foundations

- **Empiricism.** Knowledge comes from experience and decisions are
  based on what is observed. Plans don't beat reality on complex
  work.
- **Lean thinking.** Reduce waste; focus on the essentials. The
  Scrum framework is intentionally minimal for this reason.
- **Approach.** Iterative, incremental, optimizing predictability and
  controlling risk through short cycles.

## The three pillars

### 1. Transparency
- Process and work must be visible to those doing it **and** those
  receiving it.
- Decisions in Scrum rely on the perceived state of the three
  artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment).
- Low transparency → decisions that destroy value and add risk.
- *Transparency enables inspection. Inspection without transparency
  is misleading and wasteful.*

### 2. Inspection
- Artifacts and progress toward goals are inspected **frequently and
  diligently** to detect undesirable variance.
- The five Scrum events provide the cadence (Sprint, Sprint
  Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective).
- *Inspection enables adaptation. Inspection without adaptation is
  pointless.* Events are designed to **provoke** change.

### 3. Adaptation
- If process or product is unacceptable, adjust **as soon as
  possible** to minimize further deviation.
- Adaptation breaks down when people are **not empowered** or **not
  self-managing**. Both are prerequisites.
- A Scrum Team adapts the moment it learns anything new through
  inspection.

## When this applies

- Complex environments with uncertain outcomes
- Designing reporting/visibility systems for a team
- Diagnosing why a Scrum adoption "isn't working" — usually one of
  the three pillars is broken

## Anti-patterns

- Hidden Sprint Backlog or undocumented work-in-progress (kills
  transparency)
- Inspection event that produces no change (kills adaptation
  feedback loop)
- "We will adapt next quarter" — Scrum demands immediate adjustment
- Team has no decision authority → adaptation impossible
- Removing or skipping an event → loses the cadence that makes
  empiricism work
