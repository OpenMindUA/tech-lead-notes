# Developers (Scrum role)

**TL;DR:** Anyone in the Scrum Team committed to creating any aspect
of a usable Increment each Sprint. Inclusive label — "Developer" is
not limited to programmers.

## Accountabilities (always, regardless of skill set)

- **Create the Sprint plan** (the Sprint Backlog).
- **Instill quality** by adhering to the **Definition of Done**.
- **Adapt the plan daily** toward the Sprint Goal.
- **Hold each other accountable** as professionals.

## Inclusive scope

The skills needed vary widely with the domain. The Guide explicitly
states: researchers, analysts, scientists, specialists, and other
"developers" all qualify. The word is used to **simplify**, not
exclude.

## When this applies

- Defining job descriptions for non-programmers in a Scrum context
  (data scientists, designers, hardware engineers)
- Onboarding new team members — explain the four accountabilities
- Resolving "is this our work?" questions — if it produces or
  protects the Increment, the Developers do it

## Anti-patterns

- "Developer" used to exclude QA, designers, ops, etc. on the team
- Sprint plan handed to Developers instead of created by them
- "Done" defined externally with no team buy-in or no DoD at all
- Plan locked at the start of the Sprint and not adapted daily
- "Senior" Developers excluded from accountability for quality
