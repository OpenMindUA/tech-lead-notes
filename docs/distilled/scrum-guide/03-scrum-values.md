# Scrum Values

**TL;DR:** Five values that make Scrum's empiricism work in practice:
**Commitment · Focus · Openness · Respect · Courage**. Without them
the three pillars collapse and Scrum becomes ceremony.

## The five values

- **Commitment** — to the team's goals and to supporting each other.
- **Focus** — primarily on the Sprint's work, toward those goals.
- **Openness** — about the work and the challenges, with the team
  and with stakeholders.
- **Respect** — for team members as capable independent
  professionals; mutually with the people they work with.
- **Courage** — to do the right thing, to work on tough problems.

## Why they matter

Values shape **decisions, steps and the way Scrum is used**. Every
choice should reinforce them, not diminish them. When the team
embodies the values, the three pillars (transparency, inspection,
adaptation) "come to life" and **trust** is built.

## When this applies

- Designing team agreements / working agreements
- Diagnosing dysfunction (which value is being violated?)
- Hiring and onboarding into a Scrum environment
- Coaching conversations during a retrospective

## Anti-patterns

- Multitasking across many initiatives (violates **focus**)
- Hiding bad news from PO or stakeholders (violates **openness**)
- Skipping the Sprint Goal (violates **commitment**)
- Manager imposing solutions on the team (violates **respect**)
- Avoiding hard conversations or risky technical work (violates
  **courage**)
- Treating the values as a poster on the wall — not as decision
  criteria
