# Stakeholders — performance domain

**TL;DR:** Five-step continuous loop —
*Identify → Understand & analyze → Prioritize → Engage → Monitor* —
covering both internal and external stakeholders. Modern approaches
balance both; Agile-only or "external-only" focus is incomplete.

## Five steps (continuous)

### 1. Identify
- Spend serious time at kickoff on key stakeholders. Missing one
  (e.g. a regulator) can kill the project after spend.
- **Repeat continuously** — you missed some, and the environment
  introduces new ones.
- Use prior similar projects' stakeholder lists as a checklist.
- Check methodology — add identification activities if it's thin.

### 2. Understand and analyze
For each new stakeholder answer:
- How can they impact us? Power level?
- Feeling toward project: positive / negative?
- Their expectations and what they care about?

Output → engagement plan. **Justification rule:** engagement effort
< gain from support (and < opportunity cost of doing something
else). Override: ethics — invest in low-power stakeholders when
ethics demands.

Document in a **stakeholder register** (or in a generic artifact
your method provides).

Plan two information flows:
- **You → them:** keep informed; concealment is risky and unethical.
- **Them → you:** ask actively for their views; use creative channels.

Channel choice:
- formal vs informal (per stakeholder type)
- pull (dashboard) vs push (report) — pull is cheaper but easily forgotten
- medium per situation (email / printed / face-to-face)

### 3. Prioritize
Sort by justifiability of effort × power. Allows graceful
de-prioritization in crunch. Re-prioritize as project progresses.

### 4. Engage
Beyond the plan: ad hoc decisions need negotiation and conflict
resolution. Invest in soft-skill training for the PM and key team.

**Ethics is non-negotiable.** Bribery is out, even where common.
Watch for borderline patterns (paid lunches with a regulator vs
a partner manager — same gesture, very different perception).
Respect organizational and country constraints on engagement.

### 5. Monitor
- Stakeholder management deals with human perception → must be
  *adaptive*. Start with a simple plan, run, observe, refine.
- Monitor stakeholders themselves: power and interest shift.
- Recurring satisfaction surveys (e.g. P3.express monthly) — port
  this idea to other methodologies.

## When this domain matters more

- Many or politically sensitive stakeholders
- Regulated or public-facing projects
- Programs with cross-team dependencies
- Past projects damaged by missing stakeholder concerns

## Anti-patterns

- One-shot stakeholder identification at kickoff
- "Customer = stakeholder" mental model
- Push communication only; no listening loop
- Concealing information to "keep things simple"
- Long-running engagement plan without monitoring effectiveness
- Treating bribery as locally acceptable
