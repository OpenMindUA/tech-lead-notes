# Hats, not roles

**TL;DR:** micro.P3.express replaces P3.express's named roles with **four hats**, each owning a *set of concerns*. Hats don't grant authority — they assign **responsibility for making sure those concerns aren't neglected**. One person can wear several hats at once and **must rotate** consciously between them.

## The four hats

| Hat | Concerns |
|---|---|
| **Project Manager Hat** | Way of work, coordination, facilitation, problem-solving |
| **Investor Hat** | Return on investment, opportunity cost |
| **Creator Hat** | Viability of the project's output, applicable standards |
| **User Hat** | Needs and expectations of customer + end users |

## The hat rules

1. **Only one person wears each hat at a time.** Concerns shouldn't be diffused — there must be one accountable wearer.
2. **A hat ≠ extra authority.** It's a **responsibility-to-watch**, not a power-to-decide.
3. **One person can wear multiple hats** — common in 1-2-person projects. They must **switch consciously** so no concern is neglected.
4. Hats are distributed at **A2** (Project Initiation), based on team members' skills.
5. **Rotate** — micro defines explicit hat-swap moments at **E4** (each Weekly Closure) and **F4** (Project Closure).

## Why hats and not roles

P3.express has named roles (Sponsor, PM, Team Leaders, Supplier PMs) that map onto org hierarchy. In a 1-7-person micro-project there isn't enough hierarchy to support that structure. What matters is making sure:

- Way-of-work isn't ignored (PM Hat)
- Money / opportunity cost is tracked (Investor Hat)
- Output quality + standards are enforced (Creator Hat)
- Users' needs aren't lost (User Hat)

## Mapping to other frameworks (synthesis)

| Hat | Closest equivalent in... |
|---|---|
| Project Manager Hat | P3.express PM · Scrum Master · DSDM PM · PMBOK 7 PM |
| Investor Hat | P3.express Sponsor · PRINCE2 Executive · DSDM Business Sponsor · Scrum Product Owner (partially) |
| Creator Hat | DSDM Solution Developer/Tester · Scrum Developer · PRINCE2 Team Manager |
| User Hat | DSDM Business Ambassador · PRINCE2 Senior User · Scrum Product Owner (the user-rep aspect) |

## When this applies

- Setting up a 1-7-person project on micro.P3.express
- Single-person projects — one person wears all 4 hats and must swap consciously
- Coaching teams away from "everyone does everything ad-hoc" toward at-least-named-concerns
- Hat-rotation conversations at Weekly Closure (E4) or Project Closure (F4)

## Anti-patterns

- **Treating hats as authority** — they're responsibilities, not seniority
- **Multiple people wearing the same hat at the same time** — diffuses ownership
- **Single person wears Investor + PM** without rotating — short-term-only thinking dominates
- **No User Hat** — output ships without user-need check (common in tech-only teams)
- **Never rotating** despite E4 / F4 prompts — entrenches one mental model
- **Hats assigned by job title** rather than by skills + availability
