# Purpose and key departures from SG2020

**TL;DR:** The Expansion Pack (SGEP) is **not** the immutable Scrum
Guide. It explains the *what* and *why* "for the current times",
**evolves regularly**, and **explicitly departs** from SG2020 in
several places — those departures are flagged in the source.

## Purpose

- Explain the **what** and **why** of each Scrum element from a 2025+
  perspective.
- Assume reader **fluency with Scrum**; recommends reading SG2020 first.
- Includes references for further exploration.
- Targets product delivery by self-managing teams across many
  domains (engineers, programmers, researchers, analysts, lawyers,
  marketers, scientists).

## Key departures from SG2020 (catalog)

| # | Element | SG2020 | SGEP |
|---|---------|--------|------|
| 1 | Stakeholder | mentioned 14 times, never defined | **defined as a 4th role** with subtypes |
| 2 | Supporter | not a concept | **introduced** as a Stakeholder subtype / change agent |
| 3 | "Developer" | umbrella term | **renamed Product Developer**; may be human or automated |
| 4 | AI | not addressed | **explicitly covered** as participant ("human in the loop") |
| 5 | Product | implicit | **explicit artifact**; can be experience or platform |
| 6 | Product Vision | not present | **introduced** alongside Product Goal |
| 7 | Value | generic | **stakeholder value** — perceived need; assumption until evidence |
| 8 | Outcome ↔ Output | not separated | **two distinct commitments**: Definition of Outcome Done (Product level) + Definition of Output Done (Increment level — renamed from DoD) |
| 9 | Acceptance Criteria | not addressed | **explicitly defined** (with Outcome Criteria as subset) |
| 10 | Refinement | brief | **dedicated section**; can be formal event |
| 11 | Sprint Goal ownership | Developers | **whole Scrum Team** |
| 12 | Risk | not directly defined | **defined**; Scrum supports proactive risk management |
| 13 | Theory | empiricism + lean | **expanded**: complexity, emergence, self-management, professionalism, plus systems/product/discovery/leadership/first-principles/people-and-change |
| 14 | Self-management | mentioned | **explicit theory section**; bounded autonomy |
| 15 | Scrum Master accountabilities | three categories of service | **broader** — change agent across organization, AI integration, etc. |

## Stance

- Practitioners "should adopt Scrum **when appropriate**, with
  agency, urgency, courage, transparency, inspection, adaptation,
  rhythm, and resilience".
- Inspect-and-adapt should be **valued more than keeping promises** or
  serving the wrong stakeholders.
- All assumptions **can be wrong**.

## When this applies

- Choosing between SG2020 and SGEP as your team reference
- Mapping existing Scrum practice to SGEP's broader concept set
- Coaching teams that have outgrown the bare framework
- Stakeholder-heavy product orgs with ambiguous "stakeholder" usage

## Anti-patterns

- Treating SGEP as a replacement for the Scrum Guide (it is **an
  expansion**)
- Adopting all SGEP concepts at once without tailoring
- Ignoring the explicit departures — copying SG2020 ceremonies
  on top of SGEP terminology
- Using SGEP as cover for cherry-picking practices
