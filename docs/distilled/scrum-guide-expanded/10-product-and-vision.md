# Product (artifact) and Product Vision

**TL;DR:** SGE introduces **Product** as a **separate artifact**
distinct from the Increment, and adds **Product Vision** as an
optional but supported long-term concept. SG2020 had neither
explicitly.

## Product (artifact)

> "A holistic experience or a platform. Service, physical, digital,
> or hybrid, delivering continuous value to Stakeholders."

### Two flavors
- **Experience** — solution that meets stakeholder needs;
  direct interaction; solves a problem/opportunity.
- **Platform** — architectural device / foundational infrastructure
  / tools enabling other Products to be built; scalability,
  reliability, flexibility for engineers.

### Properties
- **Evolutionary** and often **long-lived**.
- Single Scrum Team and Stakeholders need clarity at all times on
  **what the Product is**, **who the customers/users/decision-makers
  are**, and **what type** of Product it is (different types →
  different stakeholders + value patterns).
- **One Product → one Product Backlog** to maximize transparency
  and value.
- "**Manifestation of *why* we do *what* we do.**"

### Health rules of thumb (Product creates and maintains traction when)

- Sufficiently addresses **satisfaction gaps**
- Is **valuable, desirable, viable, usable, feasible, safe, secure**
- Has **professionalism built in**
- Has **compelling, clear, outcome-metric-oriented Product Vision +
  strategy + goal** — often including **intent, rationale,
  anti-goals**
- Adapts and improves to **identify, represent, measure emergence**
- Is **extendable and maintainable**

### Commitment
- The Product's commitment is the **Definition of Outcome Done**
  (see separate card).

## Product Vision (optional, supported)

- A **meaningful, long-term set of valuable desired outcomes**.
- The medium-term **Product Goal is often a stepping stone toward a
  long-term Vision**.
- "**Often a work of fiction**; none of it may be true." — forming
  hypotheses and running experiments in a direction is essential.
- Vision can be inspiring **but overwhelming** — Product Goal
  reduces overwhelm by being a tangible vertical slice.
- Inspect-and-adapt applies to the Vision too — **be open to
  adapting it** as evidence emerges.
- Several Product Goals are often achieved sequentially toward one
  Vision.

## When this applies

- Establishing the boundary of "the Product" for a new initiative
- Distinguishing Product organization from project organization
- Designing measurable Product strategy / goals / vision
- Multi-team Product setup — single Product Goal, single Backlog,
  shared Definitions

## Anti-patterns

- Treating Increment as the Product (they're distinct: Increment is
  output; Product creates outcomes once released)
- One Scrum Team across many Products — feature factory risk
- Product Vision treated as fixed truth, not hypothesis
- Vision so abstract it can't inform the next Product Goal
- Product without measurable Definition of Outcome Done
