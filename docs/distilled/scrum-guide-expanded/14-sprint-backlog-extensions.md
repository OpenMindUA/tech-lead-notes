# Sprint Backlog and Sprint Goal — SGE extensions

**TL;DR:** Sprint Backlog still by-and-for the Product Developers.
**Major change**: the **Sprint Goal commitment is now owned by the
whole Scrum Team** (SG2020 had "Developers"). SGE re-introduces the
"start with one or two PBIs" guidance. Multiple coherent Sprint
objectives are tolerated when carefully managed.

## What stays from SG2020

- Three parts: **why** (Sprint Goal), **what** (selected PBIs / forecast),
  **how** (actionable plan).
- Plan **by and for the Product Developers**.
- Updated throughout the Sprint as more is learned.
- Inspected in the Daily Scrum.

## What SGE adds / changes

### Sprint Goal — ownership change
- **Created and owned by the Scrum Team as a whole** (SG2020:
  Developers owned the commitment).
- **Single unifying objective** of the Sprint, **coherent with the
  Product Goal**.
- Delivery is a **commitment by the Product Developers**.

### Coherence with Product Goal (focus rule)
- "Suppose most items in the Sprint Backlog are **continually
  unrelated to the Product Goal**. In that case, **Focus and
  Commitment Scrum Values are not being upheld**."

### Start small (re-introduced from SG2017)
- Sprint Backlog should have **enough work to get started, e.g.
  start with one or two PBIs** toward the Sprint Goal.
- Forecast updates throughout the Sprint as PDs learn.

### Multiple coherent objectives (caution)
- Multiple objectives are OK **if coherent with the Product Goal**.
- "**Be careful.** Consider the trade-off of context switching and
  focus."
- Strive for balance over time:
    - current team capabilities and climate
    - current stakeholder value
    - potential stakeholder value
    - time to value
- "(SGEP considers DevSecOps)" — security/operations work belongs
  in the same balance.

### Negotiation pattern
- If work turns out different than expected, PDs **negotiate
  possibilities within the Sprint with the Product Owner** —
  **without affecting the Sprint Goal**.
- "**No one tells the Product Developers how to size or do their
  work.**"

## When this applies

- Configuring Sprint Backlog tooling for the team
- Sprint Goal definition with the whole Scrum Team
- Mid-Sprint scope renegotiation
- Reviewing whether Sprints are drifting from the Product Goal

## Anti-patterns

- Sprint Goal "owned" by PO alone — SGE: whole team
- Most items unrelated to Product Goal — focus failure
- Sprint Backlog locked at planning, no daily updates
- Multiple incoherent objectives → context-switching tax
- Stakeholders dictate scope mid-Sprint without negotiating with PO
  + team
- Treating "DevSecOps" or security work as outside the Sprint Goal
  rather than part of the balance
