# Supporter — change-agent stakeholder

**TL;DR:** **Supporter is a Stakeholder subtype** — a **change agent**
who actively makes the organization **coherent with Scrum**.
SGE-specific concept, not present in SG2020.

## Definition

> "Supporting Stakeholders and change agents… part of a powerful
> guiding coalition who inspire and remove demotivating factors."

## Role behavior

- **Support the Scrum Team to thrive.**
- **Influence org workflows / processes / systems / products /
  services / work environment** to become coherent with Scrum
  adoption and emergence.
- Participate **when and where needed** or as requested.

## Examples (org-size-dependent)

Colleagues, decision-makers, financial sponsors, governance
oversight, managers, SMEs, marketing, HR, finance, procurement,
early adopters.

## Critical filter

> "Supporters who do **not empower** Scrum Teams to do what is
> recommended in this document **are not really Supporters**."

## Leadership expectation

- Executives and board members are critical for fostering a climate
  where Supporters support.
- Supporters **demonstrate leadership** the team appreciates —
  removing impediments, improving coherence between management
  processes and Scrum, supporting emergent change in a powerful
  direction.

## When this applies

- Designing a Scrum adoption / transformation
- Identifying who in the org **enables** vs **obstructs** the team
- Establishing a guiding coalition for organizational change
- Coaching managers into the Supporter behavior set

## Anti-patterns

- Title "Supporter" given to managers who continue to **direct**
  the team
- HR or finance departments assumed to "be" Supporters without
  genuine empowerment behaviors
- Scrum Master tries to be everyone's Supporter — Supporter is a
  distinct role with org-level levers
- Lack of executive cover → Supporters can't actually clear
  organizational impediments
