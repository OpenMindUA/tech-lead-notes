# Events — SGE extensions

**TL;DR:** SGE preserves the five SG2020 events. The notable
extensions: **Refinement may become a formal event**, Sprint
Planning's three sub-topics are explicitly framed as
**Why / What / How**, and event semantics are extended to include
multi-Product, governance, and AI-augmented work.

## What stays from SG2020 (no change worth restating)

- Sprint as container.
- Five events.
- Timeboxes.
- Inspect-and-adapt purpose for each event.
- Same time and place to reduce complexity.

For unchanged details see the [SG2020 events overview](../scrum-guide/08-events-overview.md)
and the per-event SG2020 cards.

## SGE deltas

### Refinement
- May be a **formal event** in SGE (in addition to the five). See
  [Product Backlog extensions](13-product-backlog-extensions.md)
  for full description.
- Often facilitated/supported by the Product Owner.
- Stakeholders frequently participate.

### Sprint Planning — three explicit sub-topics
SG2020's three topics (**Why / What / How**) are framed as named
sub-headings in SGE:

- **The *Why* for the Sprint** — PO proposes how the Product can
  increase value/utility this Sprint; whole team agrees the
  **Sprint Goal** (commitment owned by the whole Scrum Team).
- **The *What* toward the *Why*** — PDs select PBIs, refine in
  flight, learn capacity using **DoOD** as the quality bar.
- **The *How* for the *What*** — PDs plan how to turn each PBI
  into an Increment meeting **DoOD**; decompose to ~1-day or less
  work; **no one else tells PDs how**.

### Daily Scrum
- Same purpose; **PO and SM attend as Product Developers** when
  actively working on Sprint Backlog items.
- PDs may meet **throughout the day** beyond the Daily Scrum (same
  as SG2020).

### Sprint Review
- Inspect outcome including **emerging value validation evidence
  via Definition of Outcome Done**.
- **Definition of Outcome Done measures** can be reviewed at any
  time during the Sprint, including the Review.

### Sprint Retrospective
- Same purpose. SGE emphasizes **net improvement** as a Product
  Developer accountability — retro is the explicit habitat for it.

## Cadence note (from SGE Cadence theory)

- Sprints provide a **consistent rhythm** that enables learning,
  predictability, and continuous improvement over time.
- Cadence **builds sustainable pace** and trust between Scrum Team
  and Stakeholders.

## When this applies

- Adopting SGE's terminology (Why/What/How sub-topics)
- Deciding whether to formalize Refinement as an event
- Multi-team Product setups with shared Sprint cadence
- Integrating governance / DevSecOps work into events

## Anti-patterns

- Refinement only happens at Sprint Planning (often too late)
- "Sprint Planning is where we get goal" — Sprint Goal must be
  finalized in planning, but the conversation should already exist
- Sprint Review degenerates to demo-only — no DoOD-driven outcome
  evidence brought
- Skipping retrospective when "we're too busy" — kills net
  improvement accountability
- PO/SM in Daily Scrum as managers (not as PDs)
