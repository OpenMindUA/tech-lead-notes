# Artificial Intelligence in Scrum

**TL;DR:** AI may be a **Product Developer** or a tool, but **human
accountability stays**. Use the **"human in the loop"** model. AI
**supports** Scrum's empirical process — **does not override** it
or ethical decision-making.

## How AI may enhance Scrum

- **Empirical Process Control** — AI-driven analytics improve
  transparency, inspection, adaptation.
- **Cognitive Augmentation** — frees human team members for
  strategic, creative, ethical work.
- **Continuous Value Adaptation** — update / reprioritize Backlog
  based on live user feedback and trends.
- **Systems Insight** — surface hidden interdependencies for
  evidence-informed decisions.

## Hard constraints

- **Maintain clear human accountability for all outcomes.** AI is a
  supervised partner.
- **Human in the loop.** AI does not replace the three accountabilities.
- AI **supports — does not override** — empirical process control
  and ethical decision-making.
- The Scrum Team **remains accountable** for value, evidence
  assessment, and professionalism.
- *"At least one Product Developer should be human."*

## Concrete uses

- Backlog refinement assistance.
- Surface ambiguities in text.
- Continuous self-inspection for **bias, errors, unintended
  consequences**.
- Validate / adapt models and applications regularly.
- Ordering transparency in the Product Backlog.
- AI agents as team members.
- Test and challenge existing thinking.

## Evaluation criteria for AI tools

Evaluate **like any other contributor** to psychological flow and
learning:
- Does it **improve feedback loops**?
- Does it **help validate assumptions faster**?
- When it **acts**, is the action **ethical**?

## Adoption guidance

- Use **small, sometimes reversible trials** with safe-to-fail
  framing.
- Apply **inspect-and-adapt to AI integration itself**, not only the
  Product.
- Keep focus on **human dynamics of teamwork and collaboration** —
  AI is a potential aid for adaptiveness at speed.

## When this applies

- Introducing AI tooling into the Scrum Team's workflow
- Defining ethical guardrails for AI use in the Product
- Reviewing AI agents as Product Developers (must have ≥1 human PD)
- Evaluating an AI tool's effect on flow and learning

## Anti-patterns

- AI replaces a Product Developer entirely (no human PD left)
- AI outputs accepted without **inspection** for bias or error
- AI used to **bypass** retrospectives or stakeholder collaboration
- "We have AI" treated as a checkbox without measurable feedback-loop
  improvement
- AI integrated without **safe-to-fail** rollback options
