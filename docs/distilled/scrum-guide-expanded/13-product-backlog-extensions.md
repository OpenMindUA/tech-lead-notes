# Product Backlog extensions

**TL;DR:** SGE keeps the SG2020 Product Backlog basics and **adds
explicit treatment of Product Backlog Item (PBI), Acceptance
Criteria + Outcome Criteria, Refinement (which can be a formal
event), and richer Product Goal semantics**.

## Product Backlog (delta from SG2020)

- "Emergent, ordered (sequenced) list of **Product Backlog Items**
  needed to attain the Product Goal."
- Provides **transparency (work clarity)**.
- **Single source of work** for the Scrum Team toward the Product
  Goal.
- "A **smaller** Product Backlog often provides **more
  transparency**."
- PO orders, **always keeping value in mind**.

## Product Backlog Item (PBI) — explicit in SGE

- A **potentially valuable item**.
- **Not necessarily in any specific format** (no story format
  prescribed).
- Intended to **deal with a problem or opportunity**.
- Can include **Acceptance Criteria** (in addition to DoOD) — see below.
- Can include **Outcome Criteria** (in addition to DoOD).
- Single piece of work that **discovers or delivers value**.
- Can **evolve anytime**, even while Product Developers work on it.
- During refinement, broken into smaller PBIs the team understands.
- Occasional PBIs unrelated to Product Goal are OK; **frequent
  unrelated items = focus problem** ("feature factory" /
  "discovery factory" risk).

## Acceptance Criteria

- Per-PBI; describe **when an output for that PBI is complete** —
  in addition to the DoOD.
- Provide **unambiguous clarity on *what*** is requested.
- Functional or non-functional.
- Can **evolve anytime**, including while PDs work.

### Outcome Criteria (subset of AC)

- Describe the **intention** of the PBI — the **why behind the what**.
- Complement the **Definition of Outcome Done**.
- Form: narrative or, ideally, **measures**.
- Provide direction when questions arise.

## Refinement

- **Activity** (not a SG2020 event) — can be **formal (an additional
  event)** or **informal**.
- **Ongoing emergent process** that fosters clarity, reduces risk.
- Builds enough understanding/confidence that selected/upcoming
  PBIs are **ready** (can be Done within a small number of days).
- Considers **various types of dependencies**.
- Activities: break-down, add description / Acceptance Criteria /
  order / size; can include research (problem/opportunity validation,
  UX, solution validation).
- **Product Developers, and nobody else, size PBIs.** PO may help
  them understand trade-offs.
- Participants often include Stakeholders + Scrum Team members.
  PDs sometimes work directly with Stakeholders.
- PO can focus more on Product ownership when PDs have **broad
  Product understanding**.
- Forward-looking — clarity / direction / focus for upcoming Sprints.

## Product Goal (delta from SG2020)

- "Current single, more strategic, ambitious objective (the *why*)."
- **Medium-term** objective for Scrum Team **and** Stakeholders **and**
  Supporters.
- Often expressed as **hypotheses** about closing satisfaction gaps
  and expected business impact.
- Inspect-and-adapt: **embrace uncertainty, result feedback, side
  effects, learnings**.
- Fixed for as long as no contrary evidence emerges from observed
  Definition of Outcome Done — see [Definition of Outcome Done](11-definition-of-outcome-done.md).
- If the Product Goal **shifts often** → focus problem (perhaps
  feature factory).

## When this applies

- Designing a refinement cadence or formalizing it as an event
- Choosing how to express PBIs (story format, BDD, plain text)
- Distinguishing item-level Acceptance vs Increment-level DoOD vs
  Product-level DoOD (3 quality bars)
- Coaching teams to write Outcome Criteria, not just Acceptance

## Anti-patterns

- "User story format only" — SGE explicitly does **not** prescribe
- PO sizes PBIs (must be PDs)
- Acceptance Criteria duplicate DoOD (should add only what's
  PBI-specific)
- Refinement compressed into a single ad-hoc meeting
- Frequent Product Goal changes — symptom of focus dysfunction
- Stakeholders excluded from refinement — PDs lose access to
  domain understanding
