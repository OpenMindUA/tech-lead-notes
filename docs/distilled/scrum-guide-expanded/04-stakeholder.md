# Stakeholder — fourth Scrum role

**TL;DR:** SGE elevates **Stakeholder** to a **named role** alongside
PO, Product Developer, and Scrum Master. Stakeholders are **not in
the Scrum Team** and have **no accountabilities**, but their
classification, engagement, and governance is explicit.

## Definition

> "An entity, individual, or group interested in, affected by, or
> impacting inputs, activities, and outcomes."

Direct or indirect interest, inside or outside the organization,
its products, or services.

## Stakeholder examples

Customers, decision-makers, users, vendors, influencers, managers,
colleagues, leaders, **legislators**, financial sponsors, subject
matter experts, governance oversight. **Inanimate stakeholders**
(law, AI) should not be ignored.

## Subtypes (with examples)

| Subtype | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Customer** | Receives value by purchasing or selecting the Product (buyer / decision-maker / end-user; B2B2C and B2B2B distinctions apply) |
| **User** | Directly interacts with the Product; feedback essential |
| **Decision-maker** ("chooser", Patton) | Authority to approve / select / authorize adoption |
| **Legislator / policy maker** | Sets legal, regulatory, organizational rules |
| **Financial sponsor** | Funds; assesses viability / value / feasibility |
| **Subject matter expert** | Deep domain knowledge; supports without dictating to the team |
| **Governance** | Structures, standards, regulations that constrain and guide direction |

## Key concept — **satisfaction gap**

> "Difference between what Stakeholders experience now and what
> they wish their experience was."

The **gap to close**. Drives Product Goal definition.

## Engagement guidance per subtype

- **Customers / users:** regular intentional interactions with the
  Scrum Team.
- **Decision-makers:** "proceed with imperfect information; capture
  emerging feedback."
- **Legislators:** don't exaggerate or underestimate legal
  requirements.
- **Financial sponsors:** flexible attitude + flexible funding as
  new information emerges.
- **SMEs:** foster regular learning transfer to and across the team.
- **Governance:** single point of contact per area; intentional
  interactions; regular inspect-adapt of governance itself.

## When this applies

- Mapping the stakeholder landscape (a "stakeholder register")
- Designing communication / engagement plans by subtype
- Resolving "who decides?" disputes (decision-maker subtype)
- Setting up governance integration with Scrum

## Anti-patterns

- Treating "stakeholder" as a synonym for "customer"
- Ignoring inanimate stakeholders (regulations, AI policy)
- Single fixed engagement format for all stakeholders
- Governance not coherent with Scrum (surprise audits, ad-hoc
  contact across many touch-points)
- Fixed funding that can't adapt to new information
