# Additional theory (SGE)

**TL;DR:** SGE adds six more theoretical lenses beyond the
foundational seven: **product thinking, systems thinking,
discovery, leadership, first-principles thinking, people and
change**. Together with the foundational theory they form the
intellectual basis for the Expansion Pack's accountability and
artifact extensions.

## Product Thinking

- "**People consume Products**, not projects." Hence Scrum has a
  Product Owner, not a Project Owner.
- A **Product is long-lived**; a project is timeboxed and "leaves an
  orphaned Product behind."
- Product Owners trade off **here-and-now vs there-and-then**;
  short-term-only thinking creates technical debt, low morale,
  output focus.
- "Crossing the chasm" — strategy shift from early-adopters to
  pragmatic majority (focus on a niche; deliver complete reliable
  solutions).
- **Five Product qualities** within ethical boundaries: feasibility,
  usability, desirability, value, viability.
- **Technical debt** is shortcut-induced extra work that compounds
  like real debt.

## Systems Thinking

- Elements are **interconnected**; actions ripple non-linearly.
- Avoid local sub-optimizations (cutting unit cost while raising
  long-term cost; eroding quality goals; "improving a team or
  process that should not exist").
- Consider **upstream, cross-stream, downstream** effects of
  interventions.

## Discovery

- Not a separate phase — **continuous activity**.
- Multiple parallel safe-to-fail experiments to learn.
- Combines with delivery and value validation throughout the Sprint.

## Leadership

- "Ability to **influence, guide, inspire** a group toward a common
  goal **while avoiding demotivation**."
- **Genchi Genbutsu** — Go See, Listen, Understand; collect facts to
  inform decisions.
- Scrum strives for **leadership for resilience** — a **set of
  qualities, not a position**.
- Leadership at all levels and angles. **Anyone** can demonstrate
  leadership.
- PO and SM **balance leadership, authority, subtle control**:
  guide rather than micromanage; preserve team ownership.
- Product Developers demonstrate leadership via professionalism +
  goal orientation.
- Supporters demonstrate leadership via impediment removal and
  organizational coherence.

## First Principles Thinking

- Strip a problem to its **fundamental truths**; reason up from
  there rather than from analogy.
- Useful when challenging conventions or designing novel approaches.
- Pairs with empiricism — first-principles forms hypothesis,
  empiricism tests it.

## People and Change

- Change happens **through people**, not despite them.
- Disciplined emergent change: invite participation, run safe-to-fail
  experiments, dampen what doesn't work, amplify what does.
- "Powerful guiding coalition" of Supporters needed (Kotter).

## When this applies

- Designing a Product organization (not just a team)
- Resolving conflicts between local optimization and system goals
- Coaching managers and leaders into Scrum-coherent leadership
- Re-architecting processes that pre-date Scrum adoption

## Anti-patterns

- Project-thinking: timeboxed scope hand-off, then orphaned Product
- Optimizing one team while degrading the system
- Discovery confined to a "discovery sprint" before delivery
  starts (it's continuous)
- Leadership = the manager only
- Treating "people and change" as HR's problem, not the team's
