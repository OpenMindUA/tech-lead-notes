# Product Owner — SGE extensions

**TL;DR:** SGE keeps SG2020 PO accountabilities and **extends
substantially**: PO is a **leader for the Product**, **must be
human**, **maximizes long-term value**, owns the **Definition of
Outcome Done**, and **trusts Product Developers** rather than
analyzing PBIs themselves.

## What stays from SG2020

- Single person, not a committee.
- Accountable for **Product Backlog management** and **value
  maximization**.
- May delegate; remains accountable.
- Decisions visible in Backlog content/order and inspectable
  Increment.

## What SGE adds

### Identity / posture
- **Must be human** (PO cannot be AI).
- A **leader for the Product**.
- "Every minute spent **not trusting** the Product Developers is a
  missed chance to think more strategically, work with stakeholders,
  or create more value."
- Behaviors: visionary, customer representative, collaborator,
  influencer, experimenter, decision-maker, champion of stakeholder
  engagement / clarity / Product quality / value realization.

### Extended accountabilities (delta)

| # | New / extended accountability |
|---|--------------------------------|
| 1 | **Stakeholder engagement** — understanding Stakeholders, their power, expectations, needs, wants |
| 2 | **Continuously sense, listen, learn, adapt** |
| 3 | **Continuously balance trade-offs** — quality / speed / capability / risk reduction / value / simplicity; multiple stakeholders' competing expectations; team climate |
| 4 | **Develop and communicate the Product Goal** (SG2020 also has this) |
| 5 | **Develop and communicate a coherent Product narrative** |
| 6 | Create + clearly communicate **Product Backlog Items** |
| 7 | Order PBIs |
| 8 | Ensure Backlog transparency + understood |
| 9 | **Communicate outcomes via measures in the Definition of Outcome Done** |
| 10 | **Final say on the Definition of Outcome Done** |
| 11 | **Foster discovery, delivery, and validation of value** |

### Skills, support and step-down
- Strong **Product management skills** + **domain skills** required.
- New PO without these may need support and guidance.
- A domain SME is **not necessarily** the best PO — Product
  management + leadership matter more.
- **Step-down rule:** a PO who is neither willing, ready, nor able
  to gain Product management skills should step down.

### Collaboration model
- Collaborates with the **Scrum Team** to mutually agree on who
  does what; PO **remains accountable**.
- All Stakeholders and Supporters should **respect** PO decisions.
- PO has **delegated authority from the organization**.

### Multi-product caution
- If a Scrum Team is forced to work on multiple Products /
  platforms / projects: each of those Products / platforms /
  projects' manager should be a **Stakeholder (and Supporter)** of
  the PO. Scrum encourages staying focused on one Product to avoid
  the **"feature factory"**.

## When this applies

- Defining the PO job description in the SGE-aware org
- Coaching PO out of "spending time writing detailed PBIs" mode
- Resolving authority disputes — PO has delegated authority
- Hiring decisions: prefer Product mgmt + leadership over deep SME

## Anti-patterns

- **Committee PO** or AI PO — both prohibited by SGE
- PO as **scribe** writing detailed items
- PO without organizational backing — others overrule the order
- PO running multiple unrelated products as PO of each — feature
  factory risk
- Domain SME promoted to PO without Product mgmt skills (often
  better as Product Developer instead)
