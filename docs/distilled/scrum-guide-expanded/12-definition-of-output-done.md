# Definition of Output Done (renamed Definition of Done)

**TL;DR:** SGE **renames** the SG2020 *Definition of Done* to
**Definition of Output Done** to disambiguate from outcome (the
new Definition of Outcome Done). Attached to the **Increment**.
Quality measures expressing **due diligence** before delivery.

## Definition

> "Formally describes the quality measures that express due
> diligence for the Increment so that it could be delivered to
> Stakeholders."

## Properties (delta from SG2020 DoD)

- Typically includes **technical standards + Product qualities**.
- Scrum Team creates it if the organization hasn't supplied a
  minimum.
- Multiple Scrum Teams on one Product: **share the same DoOD as
  the common foundation**, but **may improve upon it**.
- The Scrum Team is **duty-bound to conform** **and continuously
  improve** the DoOD.
- The Increment is **cumulative**.
- DoOD is the **overall quality standard for the whole Increment** —
  **not** the per-item standard (which is the Acceptance Criteria).
- **A released Increment enables result feedback for** Definition
  of Outcome Done **value validation** — i.e. DoOD-quality output
  is the input to outcome measurement.

## Two distinct purposes

| | Definition of **Output** Done | Definition of **Outcome** Done |
|--|------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Attached to** | Increment | Product |
| **Question answered** | Is the Increment **shippable** with due diligence? | Did the released Product **realize the intended benefits**? |
| **Owned by** | Scrum Team (collaborative) | Product Owner (final say) |
| **Strengthening cadence** | Continuous improvement by team | PO + team based on emergent evidence |
| **Mid-Sprint changes** | **Not weakened** during the Sprint | Updated as evidence emerges |

## When this applies

- Multi-team product — converging on a shared DoOD
- Quality reviews and continuous improvement
- Compliance contexts where org sets a minimum DoOD
- Releasing Increments at any time inside the Sprint

## Anti-patterns

- Calling SGE's DoOD "DoD" without clarifying the rename — confusion
  with DoOD vs DoOD vs DoD
- DoOD weakened during a Sprint (use Acceptance Criteria changes
  or alternative PBIs instead)
- Multiple DoODs on one Product across teams
- DoOD never improves over time
- Treating DoOD as the *only* quality bar — ignoring per-item
  Acceptance Criteria
