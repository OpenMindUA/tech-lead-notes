# P3.express

**TL;DR:** Open, **minimalist**, general-purpose method with monthly
+ weekly + daily cycles. Use as-is, tailor gradually. Out of scope:
micro-projects (use *micro.P3.express*) and mega-projects (heavy
tailoring or another method).

- **Site:** <https://p3.express>
- **Tailoring stance:** no upfront tailoring; gradual ongoing.
- **Approach support:** predictive *and* adaptive.

## Roles (4)

- **Sponsor** — senior business decision-maker; provides resources.
- **Project Manager** — day-to-day management.
- **Team Leaders** — internal teams; in touch with PM.
- **Supplier Project Managers** — external teams; in touch with PM.

You may add new roles, but only **gradually**, based on real
feedback, without losing the simplicity.

## Process structure

```
project initiation
  → monthly cycles {
      monthly initiation (revise high-level plan, detail next month, peer review)
      weekly management (perf measurement, deviation response)
      daily management (accept deliverables, manage follow-up items)
      monthly closure (stakeholder satisfaction eval, improvement plans)
    }
  → project closure
  → post-project management (every 3-6 months for 1-5 years; benefits eval)
```

Decision gates:
- Sponsor decides *go* after project initiation.
- Sponsor decides *continue* after each monthly initiation.

**Focused communication.** Each activity group (except daily) ends
with sending a defined short message to relevant stakeholders.

**Peer review.** Project initiation and each monthly initiation —
another PM in the company reviews your work, gives feedback.
Prefer a different reviewer each time.

## Artifacts (4)

- **Project Description** — reason, budget, time, stakeholders.
- **Deliverables Map** — hierarchical product breakdown
  (mind map preferred). Each deliverable has a *custodian*.
- **Follow-Up Register** — risks + issues + change requests +
  improvement plans + lessons learned, all in one. Each item has
  a custodian.
- **Health Register** — peer review + stakeholder satisfaction
  results.

## Strengths

- Lowest ceremony cost; suitable for teams new to structured PM
- Approach-agnostic
- Built-in cadence for stakeholder engagement, peer review,
  improvement
- Combined follow-up register reduces artifact sprawl
- *Custodian* idea ensures items are tracked through to closure

## Anti-patterns

- Trying to use it on a mega-project unmodified
- Treating it as "Scrum-equivalent"
- Skipping peer reviews because "we're too busy"
