# Methodologies — distilled

The PMBOK Guide is **not** a methodology — it interprets and
enriches one. This section summarizes the four methodologies the
book covers as worked examples.

| Method | License | Approach | Project size | Domain | Tailoring |
|--------|---------|----------|--------------|--------|-----------|
| [P3.express](p3-express.md) | open / non-proprietary | both predictive & adaptive | small / medium / large (not micro / mega) | general | gradual, ongoing |
| [PRINCE2®](prince2.md) | proprietary (AXELOS) | predictive (adaptive variant: PRINCE2 Agile) | medium-to-super-large | general | upfront required |
| [Scrum](scrum.md) | open / non-proprietary | adaptive | single team, ≤10 | IT development | minimal, by design |
| [DSDM®](dsdm.md) | proprietary (Agile Business Consortium) | adaptive | large multi-team | IT development | substantial structure |

See [comparison.md](comparison.md) for side-by-side details.

## Selection rule

The choice depends on:
1. **Product nature** → predictive vs adaptive (see
   [Development approach](../performance-domains/03-development-approach.md))
2. **Project size** — Scrum doesn't scale; DSDM/PRINCE2 do
3. **Domain** — IT-only methods need heavy tailoring outside IT
4. **Coverage scope** — Scrum covers a *subset*; the others are
   broader
5. **Tailoring effort available** — minimalist (P3.express) =
   start fast and grow; maximalist (PRINCE2) = invest upfront

PMBOK 7's principles and performance domains apply *regardless of
which method you pick*.
